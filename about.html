About Me - My Journey

Welcome to my personal page! I've traveled a winding road to get where I am today, from nuclear submarines to economics, from agriculture to technology. Here's the story of how a Navy nuclear operator eventually found his way to the world of business solutions.

"Life is what happens to you while you're busy making other plans." - John Lennon

My Journey: A Timeline

2004
Educational Beginnings
Graduated from Westminster Christian Academy in St. Louis, MO. Not yet knowing what career path to pursue, I made a bold decision that would shape the years to come.
2004-10
Navy Nuclear Operator
Joined the United States Navy as a nuclear operator. Completed intensive training at Naval Nuclear Power Training Command (NNPTC) in Goose Creek, SC, learning mechanics and nuclear power plant operations. After prototype training in Ballston Spa, NY, I was assigned to the USS Houston (SSN-713), homeported in Guam, where I served for 4 years.
2011
Academic Exploration
Returned to Missouri and enrolled at Truman State University, initially studying Computer Science. Joined Alpha Gamma Rho fraternity and switched to Agricultural Science the following semester.
2012-?
Finding Direction
Left university to reassess my path. Worked delivering pizzas and at a coffee shop, where I met my future wife. During this time, I developed a strong interest in economics through reading libertarian economists, appreciating their scientific approach to understanding how the world works.
????
Career Launch
Completed my economics degree and embarked on my professional journey, which has included positions at:
  • State Street (Kansas City, MO)
  • Unyson Logistics (Maryland Heights, MO)
  • Enterprise (Maryland Heights, MO)
  • Spectrum (Champ, MO)
  • Daugherty Business Solutions (Maryland Heights, MO)
  • Currently at CGI (following their acquisition of Daugherty)

Personal Philosophy

My varied background has given me a unique perspective on problem-solving and business challenges. From the disciplined environment of nuclear submarines to the analytical world of economics, I've learned to approach problems methodically while keeping an eye on the bigger picture.

I believe that economics offers valuable insights into how the world works, and I apply these principles in both my professional and personal life. My journey may not have been linear, but each experience has contributed to who I am today.
