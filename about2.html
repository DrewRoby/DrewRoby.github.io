Pathing algorithms prior to A*

2002
The Pool Party
I don't talk about the pool party.
2003
A Night To Remember
Get my first kiss from my first girlfriend following a Sadie Hawkins dance. We are on her front porch, saying goodbye after a lovely evening.
Our lips touch, and a bird poops on my shoulder.
Textbook.
2004
Mistakes Were Made
She is beautiful and soft and willing, and like the dog who caught the car, I have no idea what to do with her.
Even if I had money and time to "date," where are all the feelings I was supposed to have? Blank is more concerning than outright bad. Out of cluelessness and desperation, I break up with her.
Some months later I proceed to ask her to the senior prom, and thereby discover the iron law of "one chance, per girl, per lifetime."
2005
Schoolyard Chums
She is unabashedly open about sex, and is the first female I can remember admitting in real life to wanting it, actively seeking it.
But not from me.
But that's fine, because I am still the dog. She can have my California roommate who takes calls from strippers at 3am, so long as he sleeps in her room. She breaks down laughing in class, because I'm scratching my back against the lockers, "like a big bear," for which I am reprimanded.
Noted: am not dog. Am bear.
2006
Good Boy
My roommate's sister trains dogs for a living, and comes to visit us in New York.
Maybe am dog after all.
2007
Neive
She is some mix of Levantine and Pacific Islander, I think. She serves at the Mermaid Tavern, and is desperately gorgeous. She never finds out I hold this opinion.
2008
On The Waterfront
I am hanging out in an expat bar in Subic Bay, shooting pool with Bobcat, when we are approached by two girls. They ask if they can join us. It comes up that they're just looking for fun, not for boyfriends.
"That's fine," I say, "we're gay."
"Oh good, we're lesbians."
It turns out we are both lying, but I spend the night watching Bobcat get increasingly frustrated with his disinterested date, while mine snuggles closer to me.
Red Horse is not, in fact, brewed with formaldehyde, but the hangover would have you believe otherwise.
2009
The Masseuse
She is the girlfriend of a friend, and is quick to tell me she does in-home services. She sits on my lap at a party, stares into my eyes, and says "You know, your lips are very... kissable." I remain oblivious.
A conversation we have many weeks later makes her cry, a lot, and we never speak again.
2009
Foreign Shores
A middle-aged Korean woman works as a mama-san, and takes a shine to me on Suit Night with the boys. She invites me to her sister's bar up on the top of Pale San Vitores, where Dong Man Lee teaches me how to shoot pool properly (get down to the table level, it's so obvious).
She feeds me with chopsticks, weird dried fish and other snacks. She brings a plastic bag full of K-soaps on VHS to my apartment, where she finds I do not have a television.
I take her back home later that night. We break up before a long underway.
2011
Sweet Grapes/Bright College Days
I should have gone up to her apartment. I regret not.
Beyond her, tied for regret, are my tripartite failures to pursue relationships with a powerlifter and two cheerleaders (not at the same time, but boy howdy).
Of the three, two were interested. The third, girlfriend of a friend; errors compounding on errors.
2012
The Penultimate Girlfriend
Art history major, sweet disposition, very innocent but trying very hard to appear very worldly. I knew her father, a good man. It takes three hours of conversation at a picnic table in the snow for her to tell me we're through.
Later I help her and her roommate move furniture out of their dorm, and rediscover the iron law.
2012
End of the Beginning
I meet my wife. We have kids. Life continues.
